Calculation Engine and Risk Quantification

The dashboard must reproduce the engineering calculations defined in the Excel model.
Each site will use input parameters to compute power, cooling, and battery margins.

The calculations must run for 9 operational scenarios.

1 Generator Net Power Calculation

Generator capacity is normally rated in kVA.
Telecom loads are in kW.

Generator usable power must be calculated as:

Generator Net Power (kW) =
Generator Capacity (kVA)
× Load Power Factor
× Alternator Efficiency
× Generator Risk Factor
× (1 − Annual Performance Degradation × Generator Age)

Where:

Power factor = 0.8
Alternator efficiency = 0.87
Risk factor = 0.90
Annual degradation = 3%

2 Telecom Load Calculation

Telecom electrical load is entered as current.

Telecom Load (kW) =
Telecom Load Current × 48 V / 1000

The telecom equipment also generates heat.

Telecom Heat Dissipation (Btu/h) =
Telecom Load (kW) × 3412
3 Cooling Capacity Calculation

Air conditioner capacity is provided in Btu/h at standard condition T1.

The real operating condition is T3 = 46°C.

Cooling derating factor:

Cooling Capacity Adjustment =
φful(T3) / φful(T1) = 0.833

Net cooling capacity:

Net Cooling Capacity (Btu/h) =
AC Capacity
× 0.833
× (1 − Annual AC Degradation × AC Age)

Where

Annual AC degradation = 1.5%

4 Air Conditioner Power Consumption

AC electrical consumption is calculated using the Coefficient of Performance (COP).

AC Power (kW) =
Cooling Capacity (kW) / COP

Where

COP = 3.5

Conversion:

1 kW = 3412 Btu/h
5 Battery Energy Calculation

Battery voltage assumed:

50 V DC

Battery energy:

Battery Capacity (Wh) =
50 × Battery Capacity (Ah)
6 Battery Usable Capacity

Lead-acid batteries:

Usable Capacity =
Battery Energy × DoD × Remaining Capacity

Where

Depth of Discharge = 50%

Remaining capacity:

Remaining Capacity =
1 − (Annual Degradation × Battery Age)

Lead-acid degradation = 20%

For lithium batteries:

Depth of discharge = 85%

Annual degradation = 10%

7 Battery Discharge Power Limit

Battery discharge rate is limited by C-rate.

Lead-acid discharge rate:

0.85 C

Maximum discharge current:

Max Discharge Current =
0.85 × Battery Capacity (Ah)

Maximum discharge power:

Battery Max Power =
Max Current × 50 V / 1000
8 Total Site Load

Total electrical load for the site:

Total Load =
Telecom Load
+ AC Load
+ Battery Charging Load

Battery charging power:

Battery Charging Power =
Charge Rate × Battery Capacity

Lead-acid charge rate = 0.5 C
Lithium charge rate = 0.8 C

9 Risk Quantification Logic

Each scenario evaluates four risk categories.

Power Supply Risk

Based on generator capability.

Power Margin =
Available Generator Power
− Telecom Load
− Cooling Power
− Battery Charging Power

Risk rule:

If Power Margin < 0 → Power Risk
If Power Margin ≥ 0 → Safe
Rectifier Supply Risk

Rectifier capacity must support telecom load and charging.

Rectifier Margin =
Rectifier Capacity
− Telecom Load
− Battery Charging Power

Risk rule:

If Rectifier Margin < 0 → Rectifier Risk
Battery Supply Risk

Battery must supply telecom load during power outage.

Two checks are required.

Power capability:

Battery Max Discharge Power
− Telecom Load

Backup time:

Battery Backup Time =
Usable Energy / Telecom Load

If either condition fails → Battery Risk

Cooling Supply Risk

Cooling capacity must exceed telecom heat.

Cooling Margin =
Total Cooling Capacity
− Telecom Heat Dissipation

If:

Cooling Margin < 0 → Cooling Risk
